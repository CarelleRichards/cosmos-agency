Mercury

Mercury is just marginally larger than Earth's moon, making it our smallest planet. Despite being the closest planet to the Sun, its surface experiences extreme temperature variations due to its lack of atmosphere.

Venus

Venus is similar in size to Earth. It has a toxic atmosphere of carbon dioxide with thick sulfuric acid clouds. Although Mercury is closer to the Sun, the temperature on Venus is about 475 degrees Celsius, making it our hottest planet.

Earth

Earth is our home and the third planet from the Sun. It is a remarkable oasis of life in the vastness of space. Earth is unique as it's the only planet we know of that is inhabited by life and the only planet with water on its surface.

Mars

Mars, also known as the “Red Planet”, consists of towering volcanoes, expansive valleys and polar ice caps. With an atmosphere composed of mainly carbon dioxide, Mars is the only planet we explored with rovers.

Jupiter

Jupiter is our largest planet. Its iconic Great Red Spot is a massive storm system larger than Earth. Consisting of mostly of hydrogen and helium, Jupiter's atmosphere harbours a complex weather system featuring vortices and intricate cloud patterns.

Saturn

Saturn is our solar system's second-largest planet. It has seven magnificent rings made of icy particles and debris. Saturn isn't able to support life. However, its moons, Enceladus and Titan, have subsurface oceans and other elements conducive to life.

Uranus

Uranus stands out for its unusual feature — the planet rotates on its side. As a result, Uranus experiences extreme seasons lasting about 20 years. Uranus has a blue-green colouration, attributed to the absorption and scattering of sunlight by its methane atmosphere.

30,687 days
Distance from Sun
2.9 billion km
Planet type

Moons

Neptune

Neptune is the most distant planet in our solar system, and it's the only planet we can't see with our naked eye. Neptune is approximately 17 times the mass of Earth, and it's atmosphere is dominated by hydrogen, helium and traces of methane.
